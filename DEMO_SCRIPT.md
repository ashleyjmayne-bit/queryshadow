# QueryShadow demo script

Target length: **2:25–2:40**. The competition limit is under three minutes. Record at 1440p or 1080p with browser zoom set so the full dashboard is legible. Use the entrant’s own voice.

## 0:00–0:18 — Hook

**Screen:** QueryShadow, Project Northstar, observed plan.

**Voice:**

> An AI research agent can keep your private document local and still leak what is inside it. The leak may not be in any one search. It is in what all the searches reveal together. QueryShadow makes that invisible trail debuggable.

## 0:18–0:55 — Replay the leak

**Action:** Click **Replay leak**. Let all six calls appear.

**Voice:**

> This synthetic agent is checking a confidential acquisition brief against the public web. Each outbound query looks reasonable on its own. But QueryShadow scores the ordered trail. A company cohort, a retention clause, an exact deal value, a churn figure, and a board date progressively consume the privacy budget.

Pause when the score reaches 91.

> The final company query supplies the bridge identity. Now an observer can reconstruct the target, approximate price, key concern, and decision window—without ever seeing the source document.

## 0:55–1:22 — Show evidence, not magic

**Action:** Click query 3, then query 6. Point to signal pills and the reconstruction panel.

**Voice:**

> This is not a mysterious AI safety score. Every finding is tied to literal evidence: the exact value, transaction intent, date, metric, or identity that joined the mosaic. The score means review priority, not proof that a provider actually made this inference.

## 1:22–1:48 — Repair the trail

**Action:** Click **Generate safer plan**.

**Voice:**

> One click produces a privacy-aware query plan. QueryShadow removes only the bridge details: exact identities become a category, exact values become ranges, and exact dates become process questions. Exposure falls from 91 to 19 while the fixture estimates 93 percent of research utility remains.

**Action:** Select two safe queries to show the rewrite.

## 1:48–2:12 — Prove it is a product

**Action:** Click **Import trace**, briefly show the local textarea, then close it. Click **Export audit** or **Copy policy**.

**Voice:**

> A developer can paste any one-query-per-line trace. Analysis runs locally in the browser; nothing is uploaded and no API key is required. The mitigation becomes an exportable audit and a policy a gateway can enforce.

## 2:12–2:35 — Codex / GPT-5.6 contribution and close

**Action:** Open **Method**, then return to the main screen.

**Voice:**

> I built QueryShadow with Codex and GPT-5.6. It helped me search for an unoccupied problem, reject a collided first concept, translate new privacy research into deterministic rules, construct adversarial fixtures, design the interface, and validate the production build. QueryShadow: see what your agent’s searches reveal together—before the internet does.

## Recording checklist

- Public YouTube video, not unlisted unless the rules explicitly permit it.
- Under 3:00 including title/end cards.
- Audible human narration.
- Show the product name.
- Explain the problem, interaction, Codex collaboration, and GPT-5.6 use.
- Avoid claims that the score proves a provider inferred the data.
- Keep synthetic-data label visible at least once.
